The Scarcity Principle

The scarcity principle is the idea that people place a higher value on things that are rare or hard to obtain. Blind boxes are often marketed as limited editions or exclusive items, which can increase their perceived value and desirability.

The endowment effect

The endowment effect is the tendency for people to overvalue items that they own or have a personal connection to. When consumers buy a blind box, they are taking a chance on a random item, but the uncertainty of the contents can increase the perceived value of the item, even if it turns out to be something relatively common or unremarkable.
